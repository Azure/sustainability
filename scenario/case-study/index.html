This fictional scenario is presented to you as an option to do this simulation exercise without utilizing your application. However, the guidelines at the bottom of this page should still be taken into account.

Contoso is a well-known and historical clothing retailer with a historically storng high-street presence. Their stores are constantly crowded, therefore joining a waiting list may be necessary to ensure entry.

Contoso’s corporate and social responsibility (CSR) “3D strategy” defines the following key goals:

  • Deeply care about their employees and their partners to build and nurture fruitful collaboration while delivering the best results,
  • Develop best-of-breed leaders to tackle key challenges of today while mitigating the ones of tomorrow,
  • Deploy the most sustainable, securely trusted, and innovative solutions to drive benefits for their company and also across society.

Currently, the retailer runs its e-commerce website on Azure where it has invested in and completed an IT modernization project approximately 2 years ago. When attending COP27, Contoso’s CTO met with a Microsoft representative to discuss the potential benefits of increasing Azure usage in alignment with their 3D strategy to help with reducing their carbon footprint and drive adoption of advanced cloud-native services. This would allow a pivot towards other innovative use cases leveraging their pool of data through the adoption of services such as Azure API Management, Azure Kubernetes Services and Azure Cosmos DB.

As a result, Contoso now aims to reduce and optimize their carbon footprint on Azure as well as reducing the administrative burden on their teams by leveraging platform-as-a-service or other cloud-native services where possible.

For a start and based upon the above, their plan is to improve their e-commerce website sustainability on Azure – that is the scope of this exercise. You work as a Cloud Solutions Architect and must consider each persona’s requirements in this exercise.

Sakura, Chief Information Officer (CIO)

  • Minimize costs where possible, particularly when service demand is low.
  • Support high reliability and availability such that there is minimal downtime for production environment.
  • Ensure that customers across geographies get the best experience when streaming video with minimal latency.

Brigitte, Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

  • Provide mechanism for different teams to manage services they are responsible for without providing more access than is necessary. Must include a way to easily review access permissions on a periodic basis.
  • Review security posture of entire environment through a single tool.
  • Ensure critical patches and updates for all services are applied automatically without manual effort.

Jaime, Chief Sustainability Officer (CSO)

  • Provide the business with easily consumable emissions metrics that will contribute to their end of year scope 3 carbon accounting report. Track progress over time.
  • Ensure that service design minimizes environmental impact as much as possible while balancing other business needs.

Ali, Chief Data Officer (CDO)

  • Ensure the data residency and regulatory requirements of each region are met.
  • Review the data lifecycle, access, and retention policy.
Tip

Review the description and start discussing solutions for the customer needs with your team. Additionally, consider the following questions:

  • What can we infer?
  • How could we address these needs?
  • Are there any additional needs that aren’t explicitly provided by the customer?
